# CDL Ticket Management Platform
## GLOSSARY OF TERMS

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** January 2025  
**Purpose:** Comprehensive reference for industry, technical, and platform-specific terminology

---

## A

### API (Application Programming Interface)
Software interface that allows different applications to communicate with each other. Used for integrations with TMS, ELD, and partner systems.

### ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
Total annual revenue from subscriptions. Key metric for SaaS businesses. Target: $2.54M Year 1.

### Acceptance Criteria
Specific conditions that a feature must meet to be considered complete and acceptable for release.

### Accessibility
Design practice ensuring platform is usable by people with disabilities. Platform targets WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.

---

## B

### BASIC (Behavior Analysis and Safety Improvement Categories)
FMCSA's system for organizing CSA data into 7 categories: Unsafe Driving, Crash Indicator, HOS Compliance, Vehicle Maintenance, Controlled Substances/Alcohol, Hazmat Compliance, Driver Fitness.

### Beta Testing
Limited release of platform to select users for real-world testing before public launch.

### Bottom Sheet
Mobile UI pattern where a panel slides up from bottom of screen, used for menus and forms.

### Breadcrumb Navigation
UI element showing user's location within site hierarchy (e.g., Home > Tickets > Ticket #12345).

---

## C

### CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
Total cost to acquire one customer, including marketing and sales expenses. Target: <$150 drivers, <$1,500 carriers.

### CDL (Commercial Driver's License)
License required to operate commercial motor vehicles in the United States. Three classes: A, B, and C.

### CDL-A (Class A Commercial Driver's License)
Highest level CDL, allows operation of combination vehicles (tractor-trailers). Most common for long-haul truckers.

### CMV (Commercial Motor Vehicle)
Vehicle used in commerce to transport passengers or property. Requires CDL to operate.

### CSA (Compliance, Safety, Accountability)
FMCSA's safety compliance and enforcement program that replaced SafeStat in 2010. Tracks violations and assigns safety scores.

### CSA Score
Numerical rating (0-100) measuring carrier's safety performance. Lower is better. Scores >65 trigger interventions.

### CTA (Call-to-Action)
UI element (usually button) prompting user to take specific action (e.g., "Upload Ticket", "Get Started").

### CRM (Customer Relationship Management)
System for managing interactions with customers and prospects.

---

## D

### Dashboard
Main interface showing overview of key information and metrics at a glance.

### Dark Mode
UI color scheme using dark backgrounds and light text, reducing eye strain in low-light conditions.

### DOT (Department of Transportation)
U.S. federal agency overseeing transportation. Issues DOT numbers and enforces safety regulations.

### DOT Audit
Official inspection of carrier's safety records and compliance documentation by FMCSA.

### DOT Number (USDOT Number)
Unique identifier assigned by FMCSA to commercial motor carriers for safety monitoring.

### Driver Qualification File (DQF)
Required documentation for each commercial driver, including license, medical certificate, MVR, and employment history.

---

## E

### EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization)
Measure of company's profitability. Target: 20% Year 2, 30% Year 3.

### ELD (Electronic Logging Device)
Electronic device that automatically records driving time and hours of service for CMV drivers.

### Empty State
UI design for screens with no content yet, providing helpful guidance and call-to-action.

### Endorsement
Additional qualification on CDL allowing operation of specific vehicle types (e.g., Hazmat, Tanker, Passenger).

---

## F

### FAB (Floating Action Button)
Primary action button that "floats" over content, typically in bottom-right corner on mobile.

### FMCSA (Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration)
Division of DOT responsible for regulating commercial motor vehicle industry and safety.

### Focus Indicator
Visual outline showing which element currently has keyboard focus, critical for accessibility.

### Focus Trap
Technique keeping keyboard focus within modal/dialog until closed, preventing users from tabbing to background.

---

## G

### GVWR (Gross Vehicle Weight Rating)
Maximum weight of vehicle including cargo. Determines CDL class requirements.

### Gamification
Use of game-like elements (badges, points, leaderboards) to increase engagement.

---

## H

### Haptic Feedback
Physical vibration from device in response to user interaction, providing tactile confirmation.

### HazMat (Hazardous Materials)
Dangerous goods requiring special CDL endorsement and handling procedures.

### Hero Section
Prominent top section of landing page, communicating primary value proposition.

### HOS (Hours of Service)
FMCSA regulations limiting driving hours to prevent fatigue. 11-hour driving limit, 14-hour on-duty limit.

---

## I

### IRP (International Registration Plan)
Registration reciprocity agreement for commercial vehicles operating in multiple jurisdictions.

### Idle State
Visual indication that system is waiting for user input (not loading or processing).

---

## J

### Jurisdiction
Geographic area (state, county) with authority to enforce laws and issue tickets.

### Journey Map
Visual representation of user's experience with platform over time, showing pain points and emotions.

---

## K

### KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
Measurable value demonstrating how effectively platform is achieving objectives.

### Keyboard Navigation
Ability to navigate and use platform entirely via keyboard (no mouse), critical for accessibility.

---

## L

### Landing Page
First page users see when visiting platform, designed to convert visitors to signups.

### Lazy Loading
Technique of loading content only when needed, improving initial page load speed.

### Loading State
UI indication that system is processing, using skeleton screens or spinners.

### LTL (Less Than Truckload)
Shipping method for freight not requiring full trailer space.

### LTV (Lifetime Value)
Total revenue expected from customer over entire relationship. Target: $1,080 drivers, $18,000 carriers.

---

## M

### MC Number (Motor Carrier Number)
Operating authority number issued by FMCSA for interstate commerce.

### Medical Certificate (DOT Physical)
Required medical certification for CDL holders, valid 1-2 years.

### Modal
UI element appearing over main content, requiring user interaction before continuing.

### Moving Violation
Traffic violation occurring when vehicle is in motion (speeding, reckless driving).

### MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
Predictable monthly subscription revenue. Key SaaS metric.

### MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
Version of platform with minimum features needed to satisfy early users and validate concept.

### MVR (Motor Vehicle Record)
Driving history report showing violations, accidents, and license status.

---

## N

### Native App
Mobile application built specifically for iOS or Android platform, installed from app store.

### Non-Moving Violation
Violation occurring when vehicle is not in motion (expired registration, parking).

### NPS (Net Promoter Score)
Customer loyalty metric measured by asking "How likely are you to recommend?" Score ranges from -100 to +100. Target: >70.

---

## O

### OCR (Optical Character Recognition)
Technology extracting text from images. Used to read ticket information from photos.

### Onboarding
Process of introducing new users to platform and helping them complete initial setup.

### Optimistic UI
Design pattern updating UI immediately before server confirms, assuming success.

### OTR (Over-The-Road)
Long-haul trucking involving interstate or cross-country routes.

### Owner-Operator
Independent truck driver who owns or leases their own truck.

---

## P

### P95 (95th Percentile)
Statistical measure where 95% of values fall below this point. Used for performance metrics.

### Payback Period
Time required to recover customer acquisition cost through revenue. Target: 3.3-5.6 months.

### Persona
Fictional character representing user type, based on research, used to guide design decisions.

### Points (License Points)
Penalty points assessed for traffic violations, accumulating toward license suspension.

### Progressive Disclosure
Design technique revealing information gradually as user needs it, avoiding overwhelm.

### Progressive Web App (PWA)
Web application with app-like features (offline support, push notifications, home screen install).

### Push Notification
Alert sent to user's device even when app is not open.

---

## Q

### Quick Actions
Shortcuts allowing users to complete frequent tasks with minimal steps.

---

## R

### RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)
Security approach restricting system access based on user roles (Admin, Manager, Driver).

### Real-time
Instant synchronization of data across all users without page refresh.

### Responsive Design
Design approach where layout adapts to different screen sizes and devices.

### RLS (Row Level Security)
Database security policy restricting which rows users can access based on their identity.

### ROI (Return on Investment)
Ratio of profit to cost, measuring efficiency of investment.

---

## S

### SaaS (Software as a Service)
Software delivery model where application is hosted centrally and accessed via internet subscription.

### SafeStat
FMCSA's previous safety rating system, replaced by CSA in 2010.

### Screen Reader
Assistive technology that reads screen content aloud for visually impaired users.

### Semantic HTML
HTML using tags that convey meaning (header, nav, main) vs generic tags (div, span).

### Service Worker
Background script enabling offline functionality and push notifications in web apps.

### Skeleton Screen
Loading state showing layout structure with animated placeholders instead of spinners.

### SMS (Safety Management System)
Software for managing safety compliance, training, and documentation.

### SMS (Short Message Service)
Text messaging service used for notifications and alerts.

### SSO (Single Sign-On)
Authentication scheme allowing users to log in once for multiple applications.

### SUS (System Usability Scale)
Industry-standard questionnaire measuring usability. Score >80 = "excellent". Target: >80.

---

## T

### TAM (Total Addressable Market)
Total revenue opportunity available if platform achieved 100% market share. $1.8B for CDL compliance.

### Three-Way Calling
Conference call feature connecting driver, attorney, and court/prosecutor.

### TMS (Transportation Management System)
Software for managing transportation operations (dispatch, routing, load planning).

### Toast Notification
Brief message appearing temporarily at screen edge, used for non-critical confirmations.

### Tooltip
Small popup appearing on hover/tap, providing brief explanation or help text.

### Touch Target
Interactive UI element sized for finger interaction. Minimum 44x44px per accessibility guidelines.

### Traffic School
Defensive driving course taken to dismiss ticket or reduce insurance rates.

### TVC Pro-Driver
Competitor platform for driver qualification file management.

---

## U

### UI (User Interface)
Visual elements and layout users interact with.

### USDOT Number
See DOT Number.

### UX (User Experience)
Overall experience user has when interacting with platform.

---

## V

### Violation
Traffic offense such as speeding, logbook violations, or equipment issues.

### Virtual Scrolling
Rendering only visible list items for performance with large datasets.

---

## W

### WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)
International standards for web accessibility. Platform targets 2.1 AA compliance.

### Webhook
Automated HTTP callback triggered by event, used for real-time integrations.

### White Space
Empty space around UI elements, improving readability and reducing clutter.

---

## Platform-Specific Terms

### Attorney Matching
Algorithm automatically selecting 3 qualified attorneys based on jurisdiction, win rate, and price.

### CSA Impact Prediction
AI-powered estimation of how ticket will affect carrier's CSA score if not contested.

### Payment Plan
Flexible payment option allowing users to pay ticket resolution fees in installments.

### Three-Attorney Recommendation
System showing exactly 3 pre-vetted attorney options, with top choice marked "RECOMMENDED".

### 3-Click Rule
Platform design principle requiring any primary action completable in 3 or fewer clicks/taps.

### Ticket Resolution
Process of contesting traffic ticket through attorney representation to reduce/dismiss violation.

### Zero-Training Interface
Design philosophy where platform is so intuitive that no training materials are needed.

## Industry Organizations

### ATA (American Trucking Associations)
National trade association representing trucking industry.

### OOIDA (Owner-Operator Independent Drivers Association)
Trade association representing independent truck drivers and small fleet owners.

### TCA (Truckload Carriers Association)
Trade association for truckload carriers.

## Compliance Terms

### Compliance Review
Formal FMCSA investigation of carrier's safety management controls and records.

### Intervention
FMCSA action taken when carrier's safety performance exceeds threshold (warning letter, investigation, shutdown).

### Out-of-Service Order
Immediate prohibition of vehicle or driver operation due to safety violations.

### Safety Fitness Determination
FMCSA rating of carrier: Satisfactory, Conditional, or Unsatisfactory.

### SMS Score
See CSA Score. SMS (Safety Measurement System) calculates percentile rankings in each BASIC.

## Technical Terms

### API Gateway
Server managing API requests, providing authentication, rate limiting, and routing.

### CI/CD (Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment)
Automated process for testing and deploying code changes.

### Database Schema
Structure defining how data is organized in database (tables, columns, relationships).

### Edge Network
Geographically distributed servers providing low-latency content delivery.

### JWT (JSON Web Token)
Secure method for transmitting information between parties as JSON object.

### Redis
In-memory data store used for caching and message queuing.

### Serverless
Cloud architecture where provider manages infrastructure, scaling automatically.

### WebP
Modern image format providing superior compression vs JPEG/PNG.

## Business Metrics

### Activation Rate
Percentage of signups who complete first meaningful action (e.g., submit first ticket).

### Churn Rate
Percentage of customers who cancel subscription in given period. Target: <5% monthly.

### Conversion Rate
Percentage of visitors who complete desired action (signup, purchase). Target: >15%.

### DAU (Daily Active Users)
Number of unique users engaging with platform each day.

### Gross Margin
Revenue minus cost of goods sold, expressed as percentage. Target: >75%.

### LTV:CAC Ratio
Lifetime value divided by customer acquisition cost. Healthy SaaS: >3:1. Target: >3:1.

### MAU (Monthly Active Users)
Number of unique users engaging with platform each month.

### Retention Rate
Percentage of customers remaining active over time. Target: >70% monthly.

## Design Terms

### Affordance
Visual clue indicating how UI element should be used (e.g., buttons look clickable).

### Card
Container grouping related information with consistent styling.

### Design System
Collection of reusable components and guidelines ensuring consistency.

### High Fidelity
Detailed design closely resembling final product.

### Low Fidelity
Rough sketch or wireframe showing basic layout and flow.

### Wireframe
Basic visual guide representing page layout and structure.

## Legal Terms

### Adjudication
Formal legal judgment or decision by court.

### Continuance
Postponement of court date to later time.

### Court Appearance
Scheduled date to appear before judge regarding ticket.

### Deferred Adjudication
Agreement where charges dismissed after completing requirements (e.g., defensive driving).

### Dismissal
Court decision to throw out charges completely.

### Disposition
Final outcome or resolution of court case.

### Plea Bargain
Negotiated agreement between defendant and prosecutor for reduced charges.

### Reduction
Negotiated change to lesser charge (e.g., speeding reduced to non-moving violation).

## Payment Terms

### Escrow
Third-party holding of funds until conditions met. Platform holds attorney payment until case completion.

### Installment
One payment in series of scheduled payments.

### Payment Gateway
Service processing credit card transactions (e.g., Stripe).

### Payment Plan
Agreement to pay total amount in multiple scheduled installments.

### PCI Compliance
Security standards for handling credit card information. Achieved via Stripe integration.

### Recurring Payment
Automatic charge on schedule (e.g., monthly subscription).

### Refund
Return of payment to customer.
